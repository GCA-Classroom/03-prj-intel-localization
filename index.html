Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel’s journey through time—how innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004—the first commercial microprocessor—igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 establishes the x86 architecture that powers PCs and servers.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 32-bit 386, enabling new levels of performance and multitasking.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel’s operational emissions peak; the following years focus on abatement, renewable energy, and efficiency.

2020

RISE Strategy

Launch of RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) and 2030 goals.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Commitment to achieve net-zero (Scope 1 & 2) across global operations by 2040.

2023

99% Renewable Electricity

Company reaches ~99% renewable electricity worldwide.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit to advance greener semiconductor manufacturing.

Intel Sustainability Focus

RISE Strategy

Under its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy, Intel sets ambitious 2030 goals, including driving industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Learn More

Commitment

In 2022, Intel pledged to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This commitment builds on decades of environmental initiatives and partnerships across the industry.

Learn More

Water & Waste

Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually and collaborates with local communities to restore watersheds. It also upcycles and recycles materials to reduce waste and advance a circular economy.
